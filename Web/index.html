How to Get Better Grades

Maybe your results were not the best, but don't worry. I'm here to help you with some interesting techniques to be the best student in your class!

Advices

  • Find a reason to get better grades. You need motivation, maybe to show your parents your education is worth it or to get an academic discount to help them.
  • Create a realistic study schedule that includes time for fun, eating, and sleeping. Write down your goals and objectives to avoid procrastination and track your progress.
  • Actively participating in class helps you pay attention and remember what was said.
  • Keep your study space clean and distraction-free. You can also try studying with other students.
  • A good night's sleep helps your brain process the information you've learned.
  • Try to emphasize understanding the material instead of just memorizing it.
  • A very important thing to remember is to stay consistent.
  • Stay organized by keeping a planner or digital calendar to track assignments and deadlines.
  • Break down large projects into smaller, manageable tasks to avoid feeling overwhelmed.
  • Take regular breaks during study sessions to maintain focus and avoid burnout.
  • Practice active reading by summarizing information in your own words and asking questions about the material.
  • Join study groups to gain different perspectives and clarify doubts with peers.
  • Utilize school resources such as tutoring centers, libraries, and online databases.
  • Stay positive and maintain a growth mindset, believing that you can improve with effort and practice.
  • Seek feedback from teachers and use it to improve your understanding and performance.
  • Balance your academic life with extracurricular activities to develop a well-rounded skill set.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy diet to keep your mind and body in optimal condition for learning.

Study Techniques

The Pomodoro Technique

Work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. Repeat.

The Feynman Technique

I think this one is the most famous technique, it consist in explaining the concept you're learning in simple terms as if teaching it to a child.

The Leitner System

Use flashcards and review them in increasing intervals of time.
